The Resolution That Worked Out

As the year winds down, standard declarations of "next year will be my year" begin. But against a track record of failed plans since 2018, one specific goal managed to beat the odds.

Nakshatra RP · Dec 4, 2023 · 3 min read

“I want to give up my bearings, slip out of who I am, shed everything, the way a snake discards old skin." - Khaled Hosseini, And The Mountains Echoed

As 2023 will be wrapped up in a few weeks, some of us have already given up on the year to improve anything about it. "2024 will be my year", we declare with hope to disguise the misery of our failed resolutions. Now brace yourselves as I drop the most coveted statement. I have a resolution that worked out!

Since childhood, I have always taken great pleasure in planning, organizing and carrying out activities. The excitement that comes with implementing a plan of action is unbeatable. Naturally, New Year's resolutions fulfilled my inner urge to create plans. From promising myself to use social media only weekly, to deciding that I'd study for two hours daily, I have had zero resolutions that were achieved ever since I started making them in 2018. And then I stopped. I started to let the years unfold freely without the burden of expectations, just like most around me. This year has changed that.

Reading was a hobby that I picked up late compared to many of my peers. I'll also gladly accept the embarrassment of revealing that the first time I completed a 300-page book was by reading Dork Diaries in Grade 6. My parents weren't avid readers and it was not unusual for me to have avoided that habit for a long time. As I got exposed to more genres in middle school, I discovered my passion for exploring the world of words. I still recall the surprise school bag check in Grade 8 when the most "harmful" item I was caught carrying was a BOOK that wasn't from the school library. The hobby continued as I traversed from one series to another to lose myself between the pages. Though I haven't been able to cover many of the All-Time-Popular-Books or reach the level of readers of my age, I still found satisfaction with the progress that I made. That was when I got into my reading slump after the pandemic. I nearly forgot the pleasures of leisure reading when I was burdened with textbooks.

In 2022, when BookTok gained popularity, I found myself getting the urge to read the titles that appeared on my Instagram page (please refrain from bullying me for being inactive on TikTok). Towards the end of that year, with riveting book recommendations from my friends, I got into the habit of reading once again. When 2023 arrived, I set the goal of reading 23 books this year. The first few months had been effortless for me. This is why I strongly believe that, perhaps the key to successful New Year's Resolutions is to start your warm-up before the year even commences. There have been times when I almost gave up on the resolution because of many other priorities. Now that the year is coming to an end, I'm proud to say that I'm just one book away from my goal.

If you've made it to the end of this post, this is your sign to choose your resolution for 2024 right now. Want to exercise daily? Start by practicing it weekly and progress to alternative days. Want to spend more time with family? Start by planning your winter holidays. Also, surround yourself with people who share similar interests. Believe it or not, you are influenced greatly by the ones you spend time with! Happy New Year in advance and All the Best <3
