Birth Of A Blogger

Finally getting a platform to express freely but realizing website builders are not really free? Solution: Vibe code it by myself!

Nakshatra RP · May 18, 2026 · 2 min read

On a random night in February of 2021, like many other ideas that pop up in my head just before sleep, came the idea of starting a blog for my writing pieces. Where did this idea come from?

Being born very reserved, it was never easy for me to speak my heart out verbally. Expressing myself needed a lot of effort, especially while having emotions that you can't name or explaining why you're feeling those. When it came to original ideas and thoughts, I always had to second-guess whether I should say them or not. Looking back, writing has been my most preferred outlet for my thoughts since a very young age. There were letters and diary entries, which later evolved into text messages and blog posts. Despite the academic proficiency I've achieved in my mother tongue, English became like a native language for me, and I found comfort in its phrases and expressions.

I went on to publish my first-ever opinion piece in Khaleej Times, a Dubai newspaper, following which I received a lot of encouragement. In 8th grade, I regularly started sending letters to the editor with my opinions on various themes provided by the Friday magazine, by Gulf News, and Young Times, a magazine for children and youngsters. I was invited to write about my journey with Gulf News for their 41st anniversary, and I was filled with gratitude to the newspaper for giving a space for my voice. Until something shifted.

The pandemic happened. My thoughts grew along with me. I craved the freedom to write independently without restricting myself to a prompt or a theme. Also, there were not many opportunities given to external contributors at that point in time. This marked the beginning of Youngistan Times. I'm not sure why I named it that way, just like how I'm not sure about a lot of decisions I made back in the day (even now). But if my younger brother attains adolescence and bothers to read my blogs, he would know how much of a revolutionary his sister was (inside her head, of course). The site was created with the help of Wix, and I had been updating it once in a while ever since. I admit the frustration of still having restrictions due to my free plan.

From describing my NRI life and adjustment, to my survival stories from school and university, Youngistan Times became my personal zone for more than 5 years, occasionally visited by others and sometimes even an exchange of kind words. Skimming across these blog posts could give a better idea of who I am as a person than spending a whole day with me. Since the holidays left me with a lot of time, I thought about taking up the challenging task of giving Youngistan Times an upgrade. With the help of AI tools, coding has become accessible to people from non-computer science backgrounds. From learning about tech stacks to deploying the GitHub site with the help of Claude, Youngistan Times has officially become my "own" site.

Writing this blog post felt like a stream of fresh water of my thoughts flowing onto the page, especially amidst the huge chunk of AI content that I've been consuming lately. Of course, I use generative AI tools frequently now to streamline my work and deliver faster outputs. I've noticed that not only is the AI trained from datasets created by humans, but there's also me here being trained by AI to frame my sentences in a genius-sounding manner (probably why it's very hard to "humanize"). Nevertheless, this is one space where my witty, reflective, and slightly sentimental style of writing will never change. It's something I've built for myself, a direct product of the voice in my head.

Dear reader, thank you for reading my rants until the end. See you soon!
