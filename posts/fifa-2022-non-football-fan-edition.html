FIFA 2022: Non Football Fan Edition

I have never watched a full sports match in my life. Then Messi happened, Argentina happened, and suddenly I was screaming at a penalty shootout with the rest of the world.

Nakshatra RP · Dec 19, 2022 · 2 min read

2022 proved to be the year of the impossibilities, and me watching FIFA wasn't an exclusion. Let me be brutally honest here - I've never watched a sports match completely, let alone football, until yesterday! I never thought I would like the game or even understand it, but the moment when Messi kicked that Adidas Al-Hilm ball past the goal line, I felt the rush of joy along with the rest of the world watching it. My internal cries synchronized with that of 3 billion hearts worldwide.

When the World Cup matches started in Qatar, I knew that this was just another event for the population to be obsessed with. In the following days, the talks about FIFA became a tradition at school. Unintentionally, I was catching up on a lot of rules and highlights of the matches. I learnt a bit of the history of games and the names of players other than Ronaldo, Messi and Neymar, understood what happens when there's a tie, and the offside rule (which I am still partly confused about). The Instagram stories and WhatsApp statuses were enough for me to get the idea of the course of the game. I found in myself, surprisingly, a tinge of sadness when Portugal lost to Morocco and Brazil lost to Croatia. Then came the exciting quarter finals. I was surrounded by Argentina fans, so naturally I grew fond of the team and its captain. However, I was never a stranger to the name "Lionel Messi", the GOAT. Argentina making it past the quarters saw the beginning of the football spirit in me. I also saw parts of the live match between France and Morocco while secretly wishing for Morocco to win so that Argentina could beat them in the finals *evil smile*. Watching the smooth victory of France raised the tensions again. But I. Don't. Regret. It.

Of course, I might not have felt the vibe more than a die-hard fan, but the Argentina vs France match played in the FIFA finals is definitely one of my greatest memories this year. The real reason for this was the sense of belonginess that it gave me. From Mbappe's two unexpected goals in (what felt like) 1 minute, to the stressful penalty shoot-out, it was one of the rare times when almost all my family members, friends (and literally everyone) were on the edge of their seats for a shared passion. The startled jumps of my little brother watching the match with me at home, the screams of the neighbourhood, the incoming pool of messages and snapshots, all made me realize that football is not just a sport. And Messi is not just a man. Vamos Argentina!
