Normalizing Mental Health Talks

Mental health has become a great topic for Instagram captions but a terrible one for actual conversations. On why we need to do better and what that actually looks like.

Nakshatra RP · May 1, 2021 · 2 min read

Maintaining your mental health and emotional well-being is as important as anything else. Majority of people find it hard to talk about it and later express their issues through unhealthy actions. Our judgmental society does not accept the fact that every person goes through internal struggles in some point of their life.

Mental health has become merely a topic for discussion, and is rarely considered in real life. All those people battling with mental health issues are considered as "unable to handle stress" by the judgemental society. Parents too, sometimes assume that it's naive to talk about problems, as they might have gone through hardships and survived without help. They seem unaware of the truth that times have changed and societies have evolved. Some people also think that it's dramatic to talk about mental health issues as it focuses on negativity. All these factors have made it difficult for people to express themselves. We live in a community which urges us to reach out for help but also has no time to help. We live in a community which judges people who can't deal with conflicts on their own.

Gender stereotypes have also influenced the mindset of people who believe that men are supposed to be tough. Men who express insecurities are generally seen as vulnerable or incompetent. According to WHO, nearly 40% of countries have more than 15 suicide deaths per 100,000 men; only 1.5% show a rate that high for women.

We have a limit for how much we would be able to solve issues for a person but talking won't do any harm! If you know anyone who's dealing with these problems, let them know that you're out there to listen. Avoid being judgmental and understand them. Advice them to seek professional help if necessary.

Let's play our individual role in the collective effort to normalize mental health talks and be there when needed!
