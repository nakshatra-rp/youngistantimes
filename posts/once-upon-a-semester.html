Once Upon A Semester...

It's been six months since I've been AFB ("Away From Blog"). These months have felt like an escape room experience, but let's see how my first semester at university actually went.

Nakshatra RP · Jan 4, 2025 · 2 min read

It's been six months since I've been AFB ("Away From Blog" - not 'Keyboard' because I have been pretty caught up with work). I must admit that these last six months have felt like an escape room experience for me. Let's see how my first semester at university went.

I've heard that the college experience is never easy, but nothing could have prepared me for the person I saw in the mirror when I went home for a week after classes ended. Already being a victim of fast metabolism, the increase in the number of steps helped to shrink the circumference of my limbs to almost those of my 11-year-old brother (or maybe he's just growing up). The first few months were the hardest. Firstly, shifting from a 4.5-day to a 6-day work week left me with no time for myself. At one point, I didn't even let myself scroll on social media because the lack of time to waste gave me anxiety. Although I adjusted to the new environment, chores and schedules, I desperately missed the freedom and time I used to have at home. I was constantly on the edge, trying to achieve side quests while waiting for one task to be completed. I slept at 10:30 p.m. sharp and woke up at 6:30 a.m. every day without even hitting the snooze button.

Nevertheless, university wasn't all about studies. We had a wide range of events, including inauguration day, fresher's day, ethnic day, open day, choir day, concert day, and so on. I was also glad to be part of more clubs and activities than I was in school. Slowly, juggling between multiple commitments didn't seem so hard anymore. However, one regret I had was not taking time for myself and my own hobbies. I rarely got a chance to enjoy the feeling of brush strokes on a canvas, the relief of articulating my thoughts in a blog post, teleporting myself to different worlds through books or hitting the high notes of my favorite tunes like nobody was listening. As I write this, I'm a solitary girl in the college canteen, connecting with my past self through an impromptu write-up.

And just like that, in the blink of an eye, my first semester ended and the next one began. Returning to this life after spending the holidays back at home was definitely a tough transition, but cheers to the next 3.5 years I'll be spending in this place. 🥂

To all those who are experiencing this transition, I hope you can relate to my journey. Remember, the best is yet to come. Happy 2025!
