A Catastrophe severe than the Pandemic

Online classes: the era when we felt like caged animals, peak laziness was normalised, and a study session somehow turned into a trip planning meeting.

Nakshatra RP · Jun 9, 2021 · 2 min read

The future generation might read the following written in their textbook (probably an e-book): "The history of virtual classes dates back to 2020, when the first online classes were conducted as an alternative to the traditional classrooms during the great COVID-19 pandemic. <Insert description of the traditional classroom here> Educators had found this model to be successful as physical conditions don't matter in a virtual platform. Since then, if had been adopted internationally and that brings us to the present, where we still use this convenient strategy of education!"

If the last sentence gave you a minor heart attack, please be aware that you're not the only one. I wouldn't want my kids and grandkids attending online classes either! The truth is that online classes had taken the wind out of our sails following an year of it's functioning. I still remember when our schools and colleges had sent circulars regarding the commencement of our online classes ("Yay we can do everything at the comfort of our home!"). Unfortunately not. We felt like animals caged in a zoo merely surviving without an idea about the future. The comfort of virtual learning had brought us to the peak of laziness. But that was an year ago. What has changed now? Nothing, I must admit.

Every student has their own definition of online classes. For some, it means "Pile of works and Zero knowledge". For others, it means "Bye bye perfect eyesight and Welcome headaches". As a teenager, I feel like a fraction of my school life has already been lost. At the same time, there's no substitute for online classes in the present pandemic. Even if there is, it is advisable not to attend due to safety concerns. Then what else can schools do? A lot, actually. Here is a list:

  • Peer interaction time: Students must be given freedom to use the virtual learning platform during breaks to interact with our friends. This will minimize the need to use the screen after classes to chat with classmates.
  • Virtual Tours: During normal times, most schools and colleges take students on annual trips. Since the arrival of the online classes, all the fun and enjoyment had departed. To simulate these, virtual tours can be conducted by teachers through websites which support 360° views.
  • Holidays: Physical classes usually get disrupted due bad weather. There was a time when holidays were given to students during rains. These holidays must still be provided during online learning as well because many students can also get affected by internet connection interruption. After all, what's more comforting than being under the blanket during a chilly rainy morning!
  • Self-Study Days: Online classes have an adverse impact on our health due to increased screen time. Schools can allot easier topics to students for self-study offline for several periods in a day or several days in a week. To ensure effective learning, the knowledge of these topics can be tested as well.

All the above ideas can only be implemented with the efficient administration of the educational institute as well as the cooperation of educators and learners. Let's extend our support in reducing the severity of this pandemic by strictly adhering to protocols and have the candle of hope ignited in us to meet each other soon! See you with the next post :)
