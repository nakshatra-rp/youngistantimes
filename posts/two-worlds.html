Two Worlds

Sitting in the lounge of Trivandrum International Airport, the familiar feeling of transition sets in. Airports serve as a bridge—a frozen slice of time separating home from a home away from home.

Nakshatra RP · Oct 1, 2023 · 2 min read

For someone who hadn't entered Youngistan Times in months, the airport was the perfect place to begin writing again. It was no easy feat to capture the essence of my holiday memories in photographs or written pieces of work. The same feeling of Deja Vu visited me as I sat there in the lounge of Trivandrum International Airport, waiting to board the flight back to my home away from home, i.e. Dubai. The pandemic made me realize how disconnected I felt from my hometown. As a result, no sooner had I stepped out of the airport in 2021 than I experienced being welcomed by the monsoon winds in Kerala. Ever since, I have never taken any summer vacation at that place for granted.

The excitement that preceded school closing this year was incomparable to any of the years that had passed by. I recalled the last day before vacation, waking up eagerly to board the bus to school only to convince teachers to give us free periods as a reward for our dedication throughout the term. After bouts of laughter with friends, the summer vibes hit me like fresh air. The next thing I remember was arriving amidst this lush tropical beauty at nightfall. On my way home from the airport, I admired the bokeh of street lights on the car window produced by the downpour and prepared myself to enjoy the last summer vacation before college. But who would've thought that these two months would flash past in the blink of an eye?

I love airports. It's a fact that I've mentioned to many people. Why did I like being at the airport? Maybe it's because of the burden that it took away from me. The burden of heartbreaking thoughts about leaving. The burden of being enthusiastic about returning. The heart's conflict to choose between two worlds. The airport becomes a bridge between these two worlds; a frozen chunk of time. I recalled the tears on my grandmother's face as I checked in. The moment had seemed like a long time ago. Now there I was, devoid of any emotions, as I heard the boarding call for my flight. I thanked that place for taking my mind off the sorrows I had sheltered, replacing them with the smell of coffee and books and the sound of people rushing. "I'll be back", I said to myself as I strapped my backpack on my shoulder, feeling the weight of switching lives. Goodbye.
